TITLE:
Pemetrexed Plus Gemcitabine in Metastatic Breast Cancer Patients After Receiving Taxane Therapy

CONDITION:
Breast Cancer

INTERVENTION:
Pemetrexed

SUMMARY:

      The purpose of the study is to determine if the two drugs can help patients feel better
      while causing the tumor to become smaller or disappear; evaluate the safety of giving both
      pemetrexed and gemcitabine in patients with advanced breast cancer.
    

DETAILED DESCRIPTION:
NONE

ELIGIBILITY:
Gender: All
Age: 18 Years to N/A
Criteria:

        Inclusion Criteria:

          -  Must have received prior chemotherapy with Taxol (paclitaxel) or Taxotere
             (docetaxel).

          -  Less than 3 different chemotherapy treatments for metastatic disease.

          -  Prior treatment with hormonal and/or radiation therapy.

          -  Must have disease that can be measured.

          -  Must be able to take care of self needs for example personal hygiene

        Exclusion Criteria:

          -  Must not be pregnant or breast-feeding.

          -  Cancer that has spread to the brain.

          -  Treatment with Gemcitabine or Pemetrexed

          -  Unable to take folic acid or Vitamin B12

          -  Treatment for another cancer within the last 5 years
